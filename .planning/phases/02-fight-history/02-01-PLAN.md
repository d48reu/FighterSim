**Narrative Templates (`HISTORY_NARRATIVE_TEMPLATES`):**

Dict keyed by `(FightMethod_value, context_str)` where context is one of: "title", "rivalry", "upset", "prospect_debut", "standard".

Template pools (at least 3 templates per method+context combination):

- **KO/TKO templates:**
  - "title": elevated language ("captured the crown", "defended the belt for the Nth time", "stopped {loser} to claim the {division} title")
  - "rivalry": references previous matchup ("settled the score", "avenged a previous loss", "ended the rivalry with a knockout")
  - "upset": flags the surprise ("In a shocking upset, {winner} stunned {loser}", "The underdog {winner} pulled off...")
  - "prospect_debut": simpler language ("earned a debut victory with a stoppage", "{winner} made a successful debut")
  - "standard": straightforward ("put {loser} away in the {round_word} round", "finished {loser} with strikes")

- **Submission templates:** Same 5 contexts. Grappler winning by sub is normal language; striker/wrestler winning by sub should be noted as surprising via STYLE_NARRATIVE_MODIFIERS.

- **Unanimous Decision, Split Decision, Majority Decision templates:** Same 5 contexts. Include "outpointed across three rounds", "survived a late scare to win", "dominated on the scorecards", etc.

Template variables: `{winner}`, `{loser}`, `{round}`, `{round_word}` (e.g. "second"), `{division}`, `{defense_ordinal}` (e.g. "third").

**Style-aware modifiers (`STYLE_NARRATIVE_MODIFIERS`):**
Dict keyed by `(FighterStyle_value, FightMethod_value)` for cases where a fighter wins OUTSIDE their primary style. Only apply these when there's a style mismatch:
- ("Grappler", "KO/TKO") -> prepend "In a shocking display of power, " or similar
- ("Striker", "Submission") -> prepend "Showing unexpected ground skills, "
- ("Wrestler", "Submission") -> no modifier (wrestlers submit people)
- ("Wrestler", "KO/TKO") -> prepend "Trading the takedowns for fists, "

**Round word helper:** `_round_word(n)` -> "first", "second", "third".

**Ordinal helper:** `_ordinal(n)` -> "first", "second", "third", "fourth", etc.
